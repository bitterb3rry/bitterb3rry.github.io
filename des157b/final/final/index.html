Notes on Inspiration

The idea of this project came about during the class brainstorming session for this project when someone put out the idea of cultural foods. I thought back to what I know about my Chinese roots and thought about all the simple ingredients and tools that are coined as the essential to cooking a meal: firewood, rice, oil, salt, and vinegar. When I was young and consuming a lot of Asian drama, I remembered one called The Season of Fate (the direct translation from the Chinese name would be 5 flavors of life). These 5 flavors are sweet, sour, bitter, spicy, and salty but there are also a couple of variations with freshness and umami.

Background Information

In the Chinese language, there are phrases to describe one's emotions in which characters that make up the phrase include one of the 5 senses of taste. (some examples) Modern English also has similar ideas like bittersweet moments, sour taste in the mouth, or calling someone salty.

Final Message

No matter who we are, where we come from, and our individual opinions, one thing we all have in common is the need for food and c’mon, we love to eat food!

Combining this all, I present Flavors of Emotion, a platform for sharing emotions in relation to a flavor.
